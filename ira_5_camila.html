Cultural Spotlight — Puerto Rico

Connected to Vistas Panorama: Chapter 5
🗺 Panorama: Puerto Rico

Puerto Rico — La Isla del Encanto

Puerto Rico is a US territory in the Caribbean known for its stunning beaches, vibrant culture, and distinct variety of Spanish. Camila's desire to explore reflects a Puerto Rican spirit of adventure beyond the island's own beauty.

🗺 Geography
Puerto Rico is a Caribbean island about the size of Connecticut. San Juan, its capital, is one of the oldest European-settled cities in the Americas, with a colorful colonial old town.
🍽 Food culture
Puerto Rican food features mofongo (fried plantains with garlic), arroz con gandules (rice with pigeon peas), and lechón (roasted pork) — all central to family celebrations.
💬 Language variation
Puerto Rican Spanish has a distinct Caribbean rhythm. Consonants at the end of syllables are often softened — Puerto Rico sounds more like Pueto Rico in rapid speech. Boricua is the proud name Puerto Ricans use for themselves.
🌴 Personal connection
Puerto Ricans are US citizens and can travel freely to the mainland. Many, like Camila, study abroad while staying deeply connected to the island's culture and identity.

Your response must include:

  1. 1Recommend a destination — be specific (a country, region, or city)
  2. 2Give 2+ suggestions using recommendation phrases — tienes que…, debes…, necesitas…, or te recomiendo…
  3. 3Mention the weather or climate — use hace calor, llueve, hace sol, etc.
  4. 4Share one thing in common — A mí también me gusta / me encanta…
  5. 5Use present progressive once — estás buscando, estás viajando, etc.
Example response

Hola Camila. Te recomiendo visitar Costa Rica.

Tienes que ir a la playa de Manuel Antonio — ¡es hermosa!

Debes llevar ropa ligera porque hace mucho calor en mayo.

También necesitas probar el gallo pinto — es delicioso.

A mí también me encanta el clima cálido. ¡Estás buscando el lugar perfecto!

📝 Recommendation Phrases — Choose 2+

  • tienes que + inf. — you have to / must
  • debes + inf. — you should
  • necesitas + inf. — you need to
  • te recomiendo + inf. — I recommend you

All four phrases are followed directly by an infinitive — the verb in its base form: tienes que ir, debes llevar, te recomiendo visitar.

⚠️ Conditional tense — off limits Stick to tienes que / debes / necesitas / te recomiendo for recommendations. Avoid sería, estaría, llevaría — those use the conditional tense which is not part of Chapter 5.
