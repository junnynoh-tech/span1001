Cultural Spotlight — Venezuela y EE.UU.

Connected to Vistas Panorama: Chapter 1
🗺 Panorama: EE.UU. y Canadá

Spanish in the United States

The US has the second-largest Spanish-speaking population in the world. Spanish speakers come from dozens of countries — and Valentina is one of them. Each community brings its own vocabulary and expressions.

🗺 Geography
Venezuela is on the northern coast of South America. Its capital, Caracas, is one of the largest cities in the continent.
🍽 Food culture
Arepas — corn cakes stuffed with cheese, meat, or beans — are an everyday food in Venezuela, eaten at any meal.
💬 Language variation
Chévere is uniquely Venezuelan/Caribbean. In Mexico, people say padre or chido. In Spain, guay. Same meaning — different words!
🎓 Personal connection
Many international students like Valentina study in the US. They navigate two languages every day — just like you're learning to do.

Step 2 — Record Your Response

Open Bongo in D2L and record a 30–45 second video in Spanish
📌 How to Access Bongo Open this assignment in D2L and scroll to the bottom. Click the Bongo video link to start recording. You are responding as if you are talking directly to Valentina.

Valentina asked you three questions. Your response video must include:

  1. 1Greet her and give your name — Hola Valentina, me llamo…
  2. 2Say where you are from — Soy de…
  3. 3State your occupation or major — Soy estudiante de…
  4. 4React to something she said — comment on Venezuela, chévere, or her classes
Example response

Hola Valentina, me llamo Jordan.

Soy de Atlanta, Georgia.

Soy estudiante de español aquí también.

¡Me gusta la palabra chévere! Es muy interesante.

¡Mucho gusto, Valentina!

💡 Remember This is a spontaneous response — you cannot prepare a full script. It's okay to pause, self-correct, and speak naturally. That's the goal.
