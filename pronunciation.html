Consonants
h Silent h Pronunciation

The h in Spanish is always silent. Do not pronounce it like an English h.

❌ hola → [HOH-la]
✅ hola → [OH-la]
✅ hablar → [ah-BLAR]
g The letter g Pronunciation

G has two sounds depending on what follows it:
• g + a, o, u → hard G sound (like English get)
• g + e, i → soft H sound (like English h)
• gu + e, i → hard G again (the u is silent)
• gü + e, i → hard G + W sound (the two dots mean pronounce the u)

g + a/o/u → hard G
❌ gato → [GAY-toh]
✅ gato → [GA-toh]
g + e/i → soft H
❌ gente → [GEN-teh]
✅ gente → [HEN-teh]
ge → soft H
❌ general → [jeh-neh-RAL]
✅ general → [heh-neh-RAL]
gu + e/i → hard G (u silent)
❌ guerra → [goo-EH-ra]
✅ guerra → [GEH-ra]
gu + e/i → hard G (u silent)
❌ guitarra → [goo-ee-TAH-ra]
✅ guitarra → [gee-TAH-ra]
j The letter j Pronunciation

The Spanish j is a strong h sound — never the English j sound.

❌ jueves → [JOO-eh-ves]
✅ jueves → [HWEH-bes]
c The letter c Pronunciation

C has two sounds:
• c + a, o, u → hard K sound
• c + e, i → S sound (Latin American Spanish)
• qu + e, i → hard K (u is silent)

c + a/o/u → K sound
❌ casa → [CHA-sa]
✅ casa → [KA-sa]
c + o → K sound
❌ como → [CHOH-moh]
✅ como → [KOH-moh]
c + e → S sound
❌ ciudad → [choo-DAD]
✅ ciudad → [syoo-DAD]
c + i → S sound
❌ cine → [CHEE-neh]
✅ cine → [SEE-neh]
qu + e → K sound (u silent)
❌ que → [KWEH]
✅ que → [KEH]
qu + i → K sound (u silent)
❌ quiero → [KWEE-EH-ro]
✅ quiero → [KYEH-ro]
z The letter z Pronunciation

In Latin American Spanish, z is pronounced like s — not like English z.

❌ taza → [TAY-za]
✅ taza → [TA-sa]
ñ The letter ñ Pronunciation

Ñ sounds like ny in canyon. It is completely different from plain n.

❌ año → [A-no]
✅ año → [A-nyo]
✅ español → [es-pa-NYOL]
rr The trilled rr Pronunciation

The double r (rr) and word-initial r require a trill or tap. Practice this sound separately — it takes time to develop!

❌ perro (English r)
✅ perro → trilled Spanish r
ll/y ll / y Sound Pronunciation

In Latin American Spanish, ll and y are both pronounced like English y in yes.

❌ ella → [EL-la]
✅ ella → [EY-ya]
✅ yo → [YO]
v/b v and b Sound Pronunciation

In Spanish, v and b have the same soft bilabial sound — not the English v. Think of a very soft b.

❌ voy (hard English v)
✅ voy → soft b/v sound
qu- qu- spelling (K sound, u silent) Pronunciation

In Spanish, qu only appears before e and i. The u is always silent — never say the u.

que → K + E (u silent)
❌ que → [KWEH]
✅ que → [KEH]
quién → K + IEN (u silent)
❌ quién → [KWYEN]
✅ quién → [KYEN]
quiero → K + IERO
❌ quiero → [KWYE-ro]
✅ quiero → [KYEH-ro]
queso → K + ESO
❌ queso → [KWEH-so]
✅ queso → [KEH-so]
gu- gu- spelling (G sound, u silent) Pronunciation

Like qu-, the spelling gu before e and i keeps the hard G sound. The u is silent before e/i, but spoken before a/o/u.

gue → hard G + E (u silent)
❌ guerra → [goo-EH-ra]
✅ guerra → [GEH-ra]
gui → hard G + I (u silent)
❌ guitarra → [goo-ee-TAH-ra]
✅ guitarra → [gee-TAH-ra]
gustar → u IS spoken (before a)
✅ gustar → [goos-TAR]
Vowels — Pure Sounds
a Vowel: a Pronunciation

The Spanish a is a pure, open ah — like the a in father. Never ay or uh.

❌ gracias → [GRAY-see-us]
✅ gracias → [GRA-syas]
e Vowel: e Pronunciation

The Spanish e is a crisp eh — like in bed. Avoid the English ay glide.

❌ me → [may]
✅ me → [meh]
i Vowel: i Pronunciation

The Spanish i is a pure ee — shorter and crisper than English.

❌ sí → [sigh]
✅ sí → [see]
o Vowel: o Pronunciation

The Spanish o is a clean oh — no ow glide at the end.

❌ no → [now]
✅ no → [noh]
u Vowel: u Pronunciation

The Spanish u is a pure oo — shorter than English. Note: u is silent in que, qui, gue, gui.

❌ mucho → [MYOO-choh]
✅ mucho → [MOO-choh]
Accent Marks & Stress
á/é/í Accent marks — stressed syllable Pronunciation

An accent mark shows which syllable to stress. Without it, stress falls on the second-to-last syllable (words ending in a vowel, n, or s) or the last syllable otherwise. The accent overrides both rules.

á — stress on marked syllable
❌ lápiz → wrong stress
✅ lápiz → [LA-pees] ✓ stress on LA
í — stress on marked syllable
❌ difícil → wrong stress
✅ difícil → [dee-FEE-seel] ✓ stress on FEE
ó — stress on marked syllable
❌ dólar → wrong stress
✅ dólar → [DOH-lar] ✓ stress on DOH
é — accent changes meaning
❌ esta → [ES-ta] (this)
✅ está → [es-TAH] (is / it is)
Incorrect Word Stress Pronunciation

Words ending in a vowel, n, or s stress the second-to-last syllable. All others stress the last syllable. Accent marks override both rules.

❌ hablo → [ha-BLO]
✅ hablo → [HA-blo]
Fluency & Delivery
Long Pauses Pronunciation

Try to reduce long pauses. Practice speaking sentences aloud before recording to build fluency. Record yourself and listen back — pause points are easy to hear.

Robotic / Flat Tone Pronunciation

Spanish has natural melodic rises and falls. Practice reading sentences aloud with more expressive intonation — try exaggerating at first, then dial it back to sound natural.

Response Appears Rehearsed Pronunciation

Your response sounds memorized rather than spontaneous. Try to internalize ideas and speak naturally from memory rather than reciting a script.

Heavy English Accent Pronunciation

Focus on pronouncing Spanish vowels as crisp, pure sounds. Daily listening to Spanish (podcasts, music, TV) will train your ear and naturally improve your accent over time.
