Cultural Spotlight — Ecuador

Connected to Vistas Panorama: Chapter 3
🗺 Panorama: Ecuador

Family & Culture in Ecuador

Ecuador is one of the most biodiverse countries on earth, and its people are known for strong family traditions. Sofía's description of her family reflects values central to Ecuadorian culture.

🗺 Geography
Ecuador sits on the equator in northwestern South America. Quito, the capital, is one of the highest capital cities in the world at 2,850 meters (9,350 feet).
🍽 Food culture
Quinoa has been grown in the Andes for thousands of years. Ecuadorian cuisine features soups like locro de papa (potato soup) and dishes made with plantains and seafood on the coast.
💬 Language variation
Ecuadorian Spanish uses vos in some regions (like ¿cómo estás vos?), though this isn't covered yet. Many Indigenous words like quinoa and cuy come from Quechua.
👨‍👩‍👧 Personal connection
Multi-generational households are common in Ecuador. Grandparents, parents, and children often live together — the concept of familia extendida (extended family) is central to identity.

Your response must include:

  2. 2Give at least one age — use tener + número + años
  3. 3Say what someone likes — use le gusta / le gustan with a clarifying a phrase
  4. 4Express a wish or hope — use desea + inf. or espera + inf.
  5. 5React to something Sofía said — her family, quinoa, or Ecuador
Example response

Hola Sofía. Mi familia es pequeña.

Tengo una hermana. Ella tiene dieciséis años y es muy inteligente.

A mi madre le gusta leer — lee mucho los fines de semana.

Mi padre espera visitar México el próximo año.

¡Me parece interesante la sopa de quinoa! Deseo probarla algún día.
