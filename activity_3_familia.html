Part 1 — Presenta a Tu Familia

Record a 60–90 second video introducing 2–3 family members
📌 How to Access Bongo Open this assignment in D2L and scroll to the bottom. Click the Bongo video link to start recording.
💡 Real or Invented? You may describe your real family or create a fictional one — a TV family, a group of friends, or entirely made up. The goal is to practice the vocabulary and grammar, not share personal details.

For each family member (describe at least 2), include:

  1. 1Their relationship to you — Mi madre se llama… / Es mi hermano…
  2. 2At least two descriptive adjectives — personality or physical
  3. 3Their age using tener — Tiene ___ años.
  4. 4Something they like using le gusta / le gustan
  5. 5Something they hope or wish to do using desea + inf. or espera + inf.
Example script

Hola. Tengo una familia pequeña.

Mi madre se llama Rosa. Tiene cuarenta y cinco años.

Es trabajadora y simpática.

Le gusta cocinar y desea viajar a España.

Mi hermano se llama Diego. Tiene diecinueve años.

Es inteligente pero un poco perezoso.

Le gustan los videojuegos y espera estudiar ingeniería.

¡Hasta pronto!

📝 Grammar Note: gustar for Likes

Use gusta + singular noun/infinitive, or gustan + plural noun.

  • Le gusta cocinar. — He/She likes to cook.
  • Le gustan los deportes. — He/She likes sports.
  • Les gusta leer. — They like to read.

⚠️ Clarifying who likes something: Add a + the person's name or relationship to make it clear who you're talking about. This is required when you switch from one person to another.

  • A mi madre le gusta cocinar. — My mother likes to cook.
  • A mi hermano le gustan los videojuegos. — My brother likes video games.
  • A Rosa le gusta viajar. — Rosa likes to travel.

📝 Grammar Note: desear and esperar + infinitive

These regular verbs are followed by an infinitive to say what someone hopes or wishes to do.

  • desea + inf. — he/she wishes/wants to…  →  Desea viajar a España.
  • espera + inf. — he/she hopes to…  →  Espera estudiar medicina.

💡 Both desear and esperar are regular -ar verbs — you already know how to conjugate them!

Part 2 — Comenta y Conecta

Watch two classmates and comment on one family member they described
📌 Before You Begin In Bongo, watch at least two classmates' Part 1 videos. Choose one and record a video comment.
  1. 1Greet them and comment warmly on one family member they described — keep it positive and specific
  2. 2Share one thing in common — use Mi [family member] también es… or A mi [family member] también le gusta…
  3. 3Add a closing comment or question — stay curious and friendly
💡 Keep It Simple You don't need comparatives yet — just react, share something you have in common, and show interest. A warm and natural tone is the goal!
Example response

Hola, Mei. ¡Qué familia tan interesante!

Tu hermano también es muy simpático — ¡igual que el mío!

A mi hermana también le gusta leer. ¡Qué coincidencia!

Mi hermana también espera estudiar en la universidad. ¡Hasta luego!
