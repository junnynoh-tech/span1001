Video Submission Rubric — SPAN 1001

🎯 25 points total
📹 Bongo Video Assignments 🎓 ACTFL Novice-Low / Novice-Mid 🌱 Elementary Spanish I

Your video will be evaluated across five areas that reflect real communication skills. Each level describes what your performance looks and sounds like — read the full row for your category before recording.

At the Novice level, the goal is comprehensible communication, not perfection. Being understood matters more than having zero errors.

Content & Completion
Did you answer all parts of the prompt with enough detail?
7 pts
Level 4 7

All parts of the prompt are addressed fully and with detail or elaboration. Meets or exceeds the time requirement.

Level 3 6

Most parts of the prompt are addressed with some detail. Meets the time requirement.

Level 2 5.5

Some parts of the prompt are missing or only briefly touched. Little detail or elaboration. Under the time requirement.

Level 1 4.5

Most parts of the prompt are incomplete or not addressed. No elaboration. Significantly under the time requirement.

To score a 7: Answer every question in the prompt. Add at least one extra detail beyond the minimum (a reason, an example, a reaction).
🗣
Comprehensibility & Language Control
Can a listener understand you? Are your words and structures used correctly?
5 pts
Level 4 5

Understood without difficulty. Minor vocabulary or grammar errors that do not affect meaning. No English needed.

Level 3 4

Understood with little difficulty. 1–2 moments where clarification would help. Rarely uses English to fill gaps.

Level 2 3.5

Some difficulty understanding. 3–4 unclear moments. Several grammar or vocabulary errors cause minor communication breakdowns. Occasionally resorts to English.

Level 1 3

Great difficulty understanding. Frequent errors cause significant communication breakdown. Frequently uses English words or phrases.

Novice tip: Minor errors in endings or gender agreement are expected and okay. The key question is: does the message get through?
💬
Discourse Level
Do you speak in full sentences? Do you try to connect ideas?
5 pts
Level 4 5

Consistently uses complete sentences. Rarely gives one- or two-word answers. Attempts 1–2 linking words (y, pero, también, porque).

Level 3 4

Mostly uses complete sentences. Occasionally gives one- or two-word answers. Some attempt at linking words.

Level 2 3.5

Mix of complete and incomplete sentences. Sometimes gives only a few words. Little to no attempt at linking ideas.

Level 1 3

Most responses are one or two words or fragments. May produce a couple of complete sentences. No attempt to connect ideas.

Easy win: Add también (also), pero (but), or porque (because) once or twice and you'll score at Level 3 or higher here.
🌟
Engagement
Did you follow instructions and show genuine effort or creativity?
4 pts
Level 4 4

Followed all instructions. Asked a follow-up question or showed creativity in the response — went beyond the minimum.

Level 3 3

Followed instructions. Attempted to ask a question or showed some creativity, even if brief.

Level 2 2

Did not follow all instructions. No follow-up question asked. Little creativity with the submission.

Level 1 1

Did not follow the instructions and/or showed no effort beyond the bare minimum.

To score a 4: Add one genuine reaction, ask a question back, or share one detail that wasn't required. That's all it takes.
🔊
Pronunciation & Fluency
Are your words recognizable? Does your speech flow naturally, without being read?
4 pts
Level 4 4

Most words pronounced accurately. Little English influence. A few noticeable errors that don't block meaning. Speech flows naturally. Response is not read aloud.

Level 3 3.5

Most words recognizable despite some English influence. No major communication breakdown. Some longer pauses, but doesn't derail the message. Not read aloud.

Level 2 3

Strong English influence. Occasional miscommunications. Comprehension affected by long pauses or flat/robotic delivery. Some portions may be read or heavily rehearsed.

Level 1 2.5

Many words unintelligible. Significant English influence. Listener comprehension is difficult. Frequent unfinished thoughts. Significant portions read or scripted.

Novice tip: Pausing to think is fine and natural. What matters is that you're speaking from memory, not reading. Practice 2–3 times before recording.
⚠️

Authenticity & Spontaneity

Responses that are significantly read or rehearsed word-for-word may receive a 3–5 point deduction from the total score. This applies to both Part 1 (your video) and Part 2 (your response). The goal is genuine communication — not a polished performance. It's okay to pause, self-correct, and speak imperfectly. That's what real language use looks like.

📋 Before You Submit

  • Record in a quiet space with good lighting so your face is visible.
  • Practice your response 2–3 times before recording — but don't memorize a script word for word.
  • Use Google Chrome on a laptop or desktop for Bongo recordings.
  • Check that your microphone and camera permissions are enabled before starting.
  • Watch your video back once before submitting to make sure audio is clear.
